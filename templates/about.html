About This Platform

RimAI

A prototype national agricultural intelligence platform for Zimbabwe, built for the AI4I Challenge 2026. "Rima" means "to plant/cultivate" in Shona.

Vision

To become Zimbabwe's centralised source of AI-powered agricultural intelligence — helping every farmer and officer make better planting decisions, predict yields accurately, manage pest risk, and avoid the crop-rotation mistakes that quietly erode soil fertility year after year.

What the MVP Does
  • Crop Advisor: Farmer enters location, soil type, and rotation history. RimAI fetches live satellite weather (NASA POWER) automatically and returns fertilizer, timing, and irrigation guidance.
  • Yield Prediction: Random Forest regression model predicting maize yield (t/ha), now factoring rotation history into the forecast.
  • Dashboard: Province-level historical yield and rainfall visualisation with risk classification.
  • Farm Assistant: A Claude-powered chatbot that already knows the farmer's latest analysis. Farmers can type questions or upload photos of pest/disease symptoms for identification.
Technology Stack
FrontendHTML, Bootstrap 5, JavaScript, Plotly
BackendPython Flask
AI / MLScikit-learn (Random Forest), Claude (vision + chat)
Weather DataNASA POWER API (free, satellite-derived, near real-time)
DatabaseSQLite (MVP) → PostgreSQL (scale)
AuthFlask sessions + Werkzeug password hashing
Roadmap

Custom-trained image classifier for pest/disease identification (replacing general-purpose vision with a model trained on Zimbabwe-specific crop photos), SMS alerts via EcoNet/NetOne, offline-capable PWA, multi-language support (Shona/Ndebele), and migration to PostgreSQL + cloud deployment.
